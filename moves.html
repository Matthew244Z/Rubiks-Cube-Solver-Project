Moves

In order to use the Rubik's Cube Solver in the home page you first need to know how to read the terminologies.

These terminologies will tell you which side and in which direction to move the cube.

Moves are separated into Basic Moves and Advanced Moves.

Basic Moves

There are six sets of basic moves, one for each side of the cube, and they are: U for upper side, D for down side, L for left side, R for right side, F for front side, and B for back side.

All these terminologies are based on a point of view. The F is the front side that you are look at, the L is the left side of the cube (where your left hand is holding).

For each side, there are three possible orientations: normal, reverse and twice.

  • Normal: move side in clock-wise direction. It is represented by just a letter, like U.
  • Reverse: move side in counter-clockwise direction. It is represented by a letter and an apostrophe, like U'.
  • Twice: move side twice. It is represente by a letter and the number 2, like U2.
